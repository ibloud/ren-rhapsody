The Ren Rhapsody

An Interactive Musical Adventure & Parkour Map built in UEFN. Inspired by the discography, struggles, and collaborations of Ren.

The Map Blueprint

Players spawn in a monochrome world. Collecting "Beat Fragments" unlocks Ren's music, dynamically changing the environment, adding color, and triggering platforming challenges synced to the beat.

Zone 1: Busker’s Alley

Gritty, urban, dimly lit. Brighton origins.

Music: "Old Boy", "Patricia's Manifesto"

Collab: Ruby Duff NPC grants the "Microphone Item" to shatter glass barriers.

Zone 2: Mind’s Theater

Surreal, psychological, theatrical.

Music: "Hi Ren", "Sick Boi"

Collab: Find floating masks representing vocal stems to solve puzzles and escape closing walls.

Zone 3: Animal Flow

Neon, jungle, fast-paced.

Music: "Animal Flow", "Illest of Our Time"

Collab: Chinchilla & Scizzahz NPCs offer a rhythm mini-game to unlock the grapple bow.

Zone 4: The Money Game

Lavish, chaotic casino.

Music: "Money Game" (Pts 1-3)

Collab: Mega Ran & Cassey D NPCs are trapped. Free them to unlock a dual-lane parkour race.

Zone 5: Symphony

Floating amphitheater in space.

Music: "The Hunger", "Road Runner", "Blue"

Collab: Every saved NPC appears on stage. Conduct the orchestra to ascend a staircase of musical notes.

Interactive Mechanics

Audio Reactive World

Using UEFN’s material editor, neon lights and glowing plants pulse, change color, and scale based on the frequencies of Ren’s tracks.

The "Ren Guitar"

A reskinned Guitar emote prop. Strumming creates a shockwave that destroys "writer's block" brick walls.

Deep Cut Easter Eggs

Walk backward for 10 seconds to find a room playing "Violet's Tale." Find a doctor's note for a "Heart of Gold" XP boost.

Build Requirements & Scope

Building "The Ren Rhapsody" in UEFN is an ambitious but achievable project. Here is a realistic breakdown of the skill level, development hours, and tools required to bring this blueprint to life.

📊 Skill Level

Intermediate to Advanced. You don't need to be a pro, but you must be comfortable with UEFN navigation, asset management, and lighting.

Key Skills: Verse programming (for NPCs/logic), UEFN Sequencer (for animations), Material Editor (for audio-reactive lights), and basic audio editing (splitting stems).

⏱️ Dev Hours (Solo)

~120 to 180 Hours.

  • Pre-Production & Audio: 10-15 hrs
  • Greyboxing & Parkour: 25-35 hrs
  • Environment Art: 30-40 hrs
  • Verse Logic & Mechanics: 30-45 hrs
  • Audio-Visual Syncing: 15-20 hrs
  • Polishing & Publishing: 10-15 hrs

💻 Software & Hardware

Software: UEFN (Free), Fortnite Creative (Free), Audacity/DAW (Free), Epic Creator Portal (Free).

Hardware: A decent Windows PC is required (UEFN cannot run on Mac). Recommended: 16GB+ RAM (32GB preferred for audio-heavy maps), solid CPU, and a dedicated GPU (GTX 1080 or better).

🚀 The "MVP" Shortcut (Minimum Viable Product)

If 150 hours sounds too daunting, you can build a stripped-down MVP version in about 20 to 30 hours.

To do this: Remove the custom audio-reactive materials, remove the rhythm mini-games, and skip the complex Verse coding. Keep it strictly as a linear parkour map where players walk through the 5 zones, and the music simply changes as they enter each new area. This allows a beginner to build and publish it in a single weekend.

Philosophy & Ethics (Respecting Ren)

Ren’s music is deeply tied to his struggles with the music industry, capitalism, and mental health. Building a map based on his art requires navigating specific ethical boundaries to ensure we honor his philosophy, not exploit it.

🟢 How This Aligns

The Underdog Comeback: Ren's story is about resilience. Using this project to learn a new skill and stage a career comeback honors that ethos.

Art & Mental Health: Translating the feeling of battling your own mind (Zone 2) into an interactive space is a deeply artistic tribute.

DIY Ethos: Using free tools to build something without asking a corporation for permission mirrors how Ren built his following.

🔴 How It Violates

The "Money Game" Irony: His song is a scathing critique of greed. Attempting to monetize his copyrighted music for personal profit via Epic's Island Funds does exactly what the song critiques.

Corporate Enclosure: Trapping his raw, acoustic, Brighton-busker soul inside a corporate microtransaction machine without his blessing goes against his desire for independence.

⚖️ Ethical Guidelines for Builders

To ensure you are respecting Ren's philosophy, follow these rules if you build this map:

  • Zero Direct Monetization: Do not enroll the map in Epic's Island Funds. Do not put ads on the site. A tip jar is okay only if framed as "support my job hunt," not "pay to play."
  • Radical Transparency: Make a video about *why* you are building it. Share it with the Ren community. State clearly: "This is non-commercial. If Ren's team wants it taken down, I will take it down immediately."
  • Focus on the Message: Ensure the mechanics highlight the *meaning* of his lyrics rather than just using his songs as background noise for a parkour course.

How to Make It Playable

  1. Finalize Core Settings: Open My Island > Game Settings. Set to "Create" mode, 1-4 players, and set respawns to "Ground Spawners."
  2. Upload Custom Audio: Import Ren's tracks (.wav/.ogg) into UEFN. Check "I do not own the rights to this audio" if publishing as a fan project to avoid monetization/copyright strikes, or submit a Rights Management Form if working with Ren's team.
  3. Configure Island Portal: Use the Island Starter Portal device and assign it to your Zone 1 starting spawn point.
  4. Publish in UEFN: Click "Publish Current Island" in the top toolbar. Wait for UEFN to compile the project to Epic's servers.
  5. Beta Test with Friends: Before making it public, invite 1-3 friends to playtest the map in a private lobby. Have them test the parkour jumps to ensure they are fair, and verify that the audio triggers work perfectly.
  6. Make Public: Go to creator.fortnite.com > Projects > Islands. Toggle visibility from "Private" to "Public" to generate your 9-digit Island Code.
  7. Optimize for Discovery: Add tags (Adventure, Music, Parkour). Upload a high-quality neon thumbnail.
  8. Share with the World: Post the Island Code and gameplay clips on the Ren Discord, r/FortniteCreative, and TikTok.
